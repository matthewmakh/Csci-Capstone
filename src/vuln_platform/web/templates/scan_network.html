Active port scanning of networks you don't own is a federal crime in the U.S. (Computer Fraud and Abuse Act, 18 U.S.C. § 1030) and has equivalent statutes in most other jurisdictions. Only proceed if you own the network (your home Wi-Fi, your own lab) or have written permission from the owner.

This tool can't tell whose network you're sitting on. Coffee shop Wi-Fi, university networks, hotel Wi-Fi, your apartment's shared landlord network — all off limits without explicit permission.
